📝 Teacher Notes - P92 ew

S1 Title: Introduce the "ew" vowel digraph that makes /uː/ (like "oo"). Today's theme is cooking stew.

S2 Hello: Peppa greets and says we're learning a new sound today.

S3 Letter & Sound: Show "ew" with big bouncing animation. Emphasize /uː/ — lips round and small like a tiny circle. Have students repeat 3 times. Compare to "oo" in "moon" — same sound!

S4 Trace: Students trace "ew". Focus on the curvy 'e' and zigzag 'w'. Encourage finger tracing on screen.

S5-S8 Words: Four "ew" words: new, few, blew, stew. Point out that the "ew" pattern is always at the end of these words. "Blew" — past tense of blow!

S9 Blending: Demonstrate blending each word. Keep "ew" together as one sound unit. For "blew": b-l-ew. For "stew": s-t-ew.

S10 Listen & Tap: Students listen to a word and tap the correct picture. Auto-plays audio with distractors from other lessons. 4 rounds, one for each word.

S11 Story: Read the short story, emphasizing /uː/ words. Ask students to make the /uː/ sound when they hear an "ew" word.

S12 Game: Stew Pot — tap each ingredient to add it to the stew! Each item says its word when tapped. Great for kinesthetic learners.

S13 Song: Sing the ew chant. Tap each line to hear it. Simple rhythm for 3-year-olds.

S14 All Together: Click any picture to hear it. Final review of all 4 words.

S15 Bye: Celebrate and preview next lesson (igh).
