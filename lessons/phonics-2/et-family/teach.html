📝 Teacher Notes - P33 -et Family

S1 Title: Introduce the -et word family. Peppa is excited about her pet!

S2 Hello: Peppa greets students and says we're learning -et words today.

S3 Family Pattern: Show -et pattern. Say /ɛt/ clearly 3 times. Ask: "Can you say /ɛt/?"

S4 pet: "Peppa loves her pet! p-e-t, pet! Do you have a pet?" Point to the dog picture.

S5 wet: "George got wet in the rain! w-e-t, wet!" Make rain gestures with fingers.

S6 net: "Daddy has a big net! n-e-t, net!" Mime catching something with a net.

S7 jet: "Wow, look at the jet! j-e-t, jet!" Point up and make airplane sounds.

S8 set: "Let's set the table! s-e-t, set!" Mime placing plates on a table.

S9 Blending: Click each icon to see letters blend into a word. Have students sound out each letter.

S10 Listen & Tap: Audio plays a word → students tap the matching picture. 3 choices per round.

S11 Story: Read the short story. Emphasize -et words in pink. Ask students to repeat each highlighted word.

S12 All Together: Review all 5 words. Point randomly and ask students to say the words.

S13 Bye: Celebrate! Preview: next time we learn -ig family (pig, big, dig...)!
